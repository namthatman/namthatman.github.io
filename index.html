About Me

Hello and welcome, as well as my sincerest gratitude to you.

My name is Nam Vu. It's so nice to meet you. Thank you for paying a visit to my page. I made this page to manage my professional profile, and showcase my career objective, skill sets, portfolio, and experiences on my path in the Data Science field.

I first studied Computer Science at HCM University of Technology, Vietnam for two years, before transferring to The University of Queensland, Australia. In 2020, I graduated bachelor's degree from UQ, majoring in Data Science.

As a data scientist, I work to develop software applications for data analytics production in business. With a strong background in IT/Computer Science/Software Engineering, plus having a huge love for the Business aspect, I strive for improving user experiences through deriving insights out of data, and delivering them (insights) to less technical colleagues and/or stakeholders to drive attentions and engagements.

Previously, I worked for Zalo, VNG Vietnam as a data scientist. I was tasked to derive insights through conducting analysis of users' behaviors on zingmp3 platform, and deploy a tool for checking spelling and grammatical errors on texts.

Before moving to the next section, thank you once again for looking at my page.


* 'Namthatman' is the code name I have been using since I first started to code at university. And till now, this name appears in all of my work, my social accounts (linkedin, email, github, etc.), in competitions (hackathons, kaggle) as well.

Python

SQL

Machine Learning

ML is the study of computer algorithms that improve automatically through experience. It is widely applied in many services like email filtering, recommender system, credit risk score, cancer detection, etc.

Jupyter Notebook

It is a web app that allows to write code, clean data, visualize, conduct data analysis, and apply statistical modelling on data to derive data-driven insights to support decision making.

Tableau

As an interactive data visualization tool, Tableau that connects to almost any database can help anyone see and understand their data by only dragging and dropping behavior.

Web Scraping

A.k.a web crawling, web harvesting, or web data extraction, the technique is used for getting valuable information from websites without manually copy-paste info to Excel file.

Experiences

This section goes through many crucial periods in my life so far, from when I was a kid to when I start my first job in the data field.

Thank you once again for reading my story.


  • 1996

  • 2003, Elementary School
    - The 'BIG' Kid

    Started school a year later compared to my age, I am naturally 1 year older than my same grade friends. Boastingly, my academic and wit were top-class. For those reasons, I always acted like a big brother to the class, a leader of my 'gang', groupie o' friends, experienced together the fun part of childhood. What a memorable time! Whether was it a good or bad thing? I would say both.

  • 2008, Junior High
    - The Elites

    With high academic achievements, I entered junior high school and soon joined the top advanced class. This class representing the face of my pride school is full of 'beast' students excelling in both academic and physical activities. Joining this class is a challenge, but not being dropped out of this class after each year selection is the hardest one. No one would want to be the dropping-out ones, as we all set that as the ultimate goal, no exception for me. Besides that individual objective, the class united as one family either on the study, school competitions, moments of joys or sadness. In the end, I was proud to graduate as one of the elites.

  • 2012, High School
    - Dream High

    In high school, I was also in the top class of the school. However, at that time, I didn't feel such tense pressure as of my junior high about staying in the tops. Rather, I put many thoughts and concerns dreaming of my future career path, what job I want to do or pursue, or what suits my abilities. I had thought about choosing the doctor profession or becoming a petroleum engineer. Later, I even attended a drawing class for the architecture's degree. And in the end, I chose to follow the IT profession as the final decision at my high school time. Why did I choose IT? Well, I can only answer blatantly that it was because of many many reasons...

  • 2015, University Entrance
    - Easy?

    After high school, every VNese high school graduates need to attend the university entrance exam if they want to study higher. The universities then ranks their contestants, based on the total exam scores of all subjects, and chooses the top highest Ns to accept their admission. In general, this is the most challenging also hardest exam for all VNese students. And then easily I passed the exam and got admission to my chosen degree at one of the top universities in the country. It was quite 'easy'. Am I kidding? Maybe Yes, or No, of course, it was the fruits, built up by the very hard working and efforts in the past years I did.

  • 2015, University of Technology
    - 'Hello World'

    Studied at HCM University of Technology, the path I chose stepping on to cultivate toward my future career as a software engineer, certainly I feel proud and happy. Two years passed by without much challenging, me and my dear friends, who look in the same direction as I do, look forward to the bright future ahead. The university would provide us with the highest standard; well-structured programs; top excellent professors/lecturers; resourceful learning materials, cutting-edge equipment, etc. Everything we need for our development into future promising employees, who will enter the industry workforce after graduation. 'A smooth procedure'. At least that was what I originally thought...

  • 2017, First Internship
    - Frog In The Well

    I started my first internship at TMA Solutions, as a backend developer intern during my university break. Excitedly looking forward to this intern as the first engagement to the industry, a real practical test of my ability. And in the end, I failed. The experience at TMA taught me that I was still a 'nothing' at that time. The knowledge, skills, everything I had learnt at university was not enough. I was completely brought back from the clouds, the picture of succession I had always imagined was torn down. I must change myself, I must go out of the comfy zone, I must learn more, I must fail more as well as get up more, I must be the ace again in order to survive in this competitive workforce.

  • 2018, Study Abroad
    - New Horizon

    After 2 years, I transferred my degree from my current university and got admission to The University of Queensland, top 50 universities worldwide, to continue the study. Left Vietnam, my home country, I went abroad and studied in a completely new country, Australia. This Koalas 'n Kangaroos land is the perfect place to get me out of my comfort zone to challenge myself, to expand my learning, and to further my competence. The place that I would spend the next 2 years living and studying in, in a serious attempt to broaden my small world.

  • 2019, Data Science
    - The Transition

    Quickly 1 year had passed, in the 4th year, also the last year of university life, at that very critical point in my life, I changed my path to pursue the career of data scientist. 'The sexist career in the 21st century, Data Science' At first, it was just a bit of 'all the fuss' curiosity about this sexist career. Then I started learning 1 or 2 courses online, following by learnt the next 3,4, or more courses, then bought some books to read, then later worked on case-studies, side projects in data science. Unconsciously, I fell in love with it and completely changed my degree to be able to attend more courses offering by UQ for data science. The data field did genuinely click with me, and I drove to learn more as much as I could from that period.

  • 2020, July, Graduation
    - The End

    Learning from university, learning from online sources, making connections with seniors then learning from them, doing own projects, attending conferences, engaging in forums, finding new materials, reading new researches, practicing latest techniques, competing in hackathons/kaggle, writing blogs to showcase my work, etc. With the goal of becoming a data scientist set in my eyes, I put much more serious efforts than ever before. I believe in hard work pays off in gold. As I followed that path to become a data scientist, time passed quickly. Finally, I graduated from UQ with a bachelor's degree of science, majoring in computer science. Started off as a freshman dreaming to be a software engineer, and then ended as a graduate fully ready to land in a data science job. I would say that it had been a long and tough journey for me. This experience contains all up and down moments in my life so far that I won't dare to forget any single moment. While this memorable journey closing marked the end to my university life and the growth of me in 5 years, the end has just marked the beginning of my career with the new journey ahead.

  • 2020, July, Zalo
    - The Beginning

    I got an interview at Zalo soon after graduated for a data scientist position. Due to the covid-19 and the travel bans, I work for them as a contractor and work remotely from Australia. In a small team of data scientists, we are tasked to delivery machine learning services into productions to improve user experiences. For me, my responsibilities include deriving insights through conducting analysis of users' behaviors on zingmp3 platform, and deploying a tool for checking spelling and grammatical errors on texts. Working for Zalo is an essential step in my career and I truly appreciate the company for this opportunity.

  • A
    Long Road Ahead!

Hobbies

The 5 hobbies that I really enjoy. Hope you find them interesting.

Running

I am quick on my feet. I love running. During my junior high and high school, I was always on the track and field team, and be selected as the top representatives to compete with other schools. My specialty on the 100-metre dash was top-class and I had proudly won many medals for the school. My best record was 13.78s at my 9th grade. Nowadays, I am not into competence as before, but I keep running as a routine to keep me fit and active.

Billiards

I honestly don't think that I am a pro at billiards, but quite a skillful player I am. Billiards is one of my favorite activity since I first started learning to play it. I always enjoy every time I play with my friends, either for a chill-friendly match or a bit of competition. Billiards is an elegant sport that requires a combination of precision, cleverness, logical thinking of applying physics-mathematics, and also luck.

Fishing

I still remember back to the first day went to the riverbank with my father, held in the fishing rod and pulled up the very first fish I catched. Till now, fishing is still one of the hobbies that I like the best. The satisfaction of catching a fish on your own is so great. I usually go fishing 2 or 3 times a year. Sometimes I fish at the rivers, other times at the indoor pools, but the best experience is fishing on a fishing boat at sea.

Gaming

To me, video games are a part of me. Firmly to say that I can learn so many things from play a game compared to read a book or watch a movie. A good video game could provide me with a variety of materials for self-improvement. For example, I learn the rich and meaningful messages via storytellings of the game; I enrich my creativity and imagination through exploring the game world; I train critical thinking by solved puzzles or challenges; I meet new interesting game characters, that later I could develop companionship, love or hatred feelings toward; I cultivate a sense of gratitude to the hardwork of the developer team after finishing a game. I wouldn't mind talking all day about my love for video games, so let stop here...

Reading

When I was young, I did not like reading at all. Thought it was boring and took times so I usually stopped half-way through after reading a few first pages. But to be frank, I really do enjoy reading recently, maybe because of the pandemic now that I have more time to open and finish a book then find the beauty inside of it. Reading also helps me realize how poor my writing is right now, and of course, it will get better soon, I strongly believe that. By the way, the genres that I like are mystery, crime and detective novel.
