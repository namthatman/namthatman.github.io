About Me

Hello and welcome, as well as my sincerest to you.

My name is Nam Vu. It's so nice to meet you. Thank you for paying a visit to my page. I made this page to manage my professional profile, and showcase my career objective, skill sets, portfolio, and experiences on my path in the Data Science field.

I first studied Computer Science at HCM University of Technology, Vietnam for two years, before transferring to The University of Queensland, Australia. In 2020, I graduated with a bachelor's degree from UQ, majoring in Data Science.

As a data scientist, I work to develop software applications for data analytics production in business. With a strong background in IT/Computer Science/Software Engineering, plus having a huge love for the Business aspect, I strive for improving user experiences through deriving insights out of data, and delivering them (insights) to less technical colleagues and/or stakeholders to drive attentions and engagements.

Previously, I worked for Zalo, VNG Vietnam as a data scientist. I was previously tasked to deliver insights based off of the user behavior analysis on zingmp3 platform, and deploy a tool for checking spelling and grammatical errors on texts.

    In high school, I was also in the top class of the school. However, at that time, I didn't feel such tense pressure as of my junior high about staying in the tops.   • 2015, University Entrance
    - Easy?

    To graduate from high school, every Vnese pupil needs to pass the university entrance exam with a baseline score. Those who want to study higher will apply to university. The universities then ranks their contestants, based on the total exam scores of all subjects, and chooses the top highest Ns to accept their admission. To be honest, this time period was quite challenging to most Vnese pupils but me. I passed the exam with a flying score and got admission to the top university in the country. It was quite 'easy'. Am I kidding? Maybe Yes, or No, of course, it was the fruits, built up by the very hard working and efforts in the past years I did.

  • 2015, University of Technology
    - 'Hello World'

    Having studied at HCM University of Technology, the path I had chose to step towards my future career as a software engineer was certainly one of the wisest decisions I’ve ever made. Two years passed by without much challenging, me and my dear friends, who look in the same direction as I do, look forward to the bright future ahead. Besides, the university provided us with the highest standard; well-structured programs; top excellent professors/lecturers; resourceful learning materials, cutting-edge equipment, etc, is everything needed for our development into future promising employees, who will enter the industry workforce after graduation. That was at least what I originally thought of 'A smooth procedure'.

    - Frog In The Well

    Being an intern for TMA Solutions as a backend engineer during the university break really opened a new and exciting opportunity to engage me in the industry. Despite being an outstanding student during the academic period, I still felt short for a couple of times at TMA. Very soon the experience there really helped me realize the knowledge, skills acquired from university were not enough, and the image of succession I always imagined was completely torn apart since my first day of the internship. Simply put, I was brought back down to the earth and shortly figured that I had to change myself and go out of the comfort zone. No matter how many times I fail, keep getting back up.

This Koalas 'n Kangaroos land is the perfect place to get me out of my comfort zone to challenge myself, to expand my learning, and to further my competence. It is the place that I would spend the next 2 years living in and studying in a serious attempt to broaden my small world.

  • 2019, Data Science
    - The Transition

    The last year at university was a turning point in life as I decided to pursue the career path of data scientist. There is an old saying 'the sexist career in the 21st century, Data Science'. At first, it was just a bit of 'all the fuss' curiosity about the career, and I started consuming 1 or 2 courses online, followed by taking the next 3, 4, or more courses. In addition, I bought some books to further my knowledge, then later worked on case-studies, side projects in data science. Unconsciously, I was totally in love with it.

  • 2020, July, Graduation
    - The End

    With the goal of becoming a data scientist set in my eyes, I put much more serious efforts than ever before. It could be listed as learning from university, online sources, and my work-place seniors, doing my own projects, attending conferences, engaging in forums, finding new materials, reading new researches, practicing latest techniques, competing in hackathons / kaggle, writing blogs to showcase my work, etc. I believe hard work will eventually pay off in gold. Started off as an undergraduate dreaming to be a software engineer, then ended up as a graduate who is soon to be a full fledged data scientist. Up to this point, it had been a long and tough journey as it contained so many up and down moments along the way that I would not forget. While this memorable moment marked the end to my university life and the growth of me over the last 5 years, it has just marked the beginning of my career with the new journey ahead.

  • 2020, July, Zalo
    - The Beginning

    Landing a job in data science at Zalo was a great opportunity to test my capability and ability that I seriously had prepared in the previous year. In our team, we were tasked to deliver machine learning services to production to improve user experiences. My primary responsibility included deriving insights through the analysis of users’ behaviors on Zingmp3 platform, deploying a tool for checking spelling and for identifying grammatical errors on texts. Having a chance to work for Zalo was an essential step in my career and I was honoured to be a part of them.
